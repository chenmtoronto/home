Stereo-lithography (SLA)

Stereo-lithography (SLA) Is the process of 3d printing that involves the hardening of resin using UV light. This method allows for high resolution objects. However, these printers often are expense to maintain and is limited to certain materials.

Advantages

  • High resolution prints, due to the high amount of detail that is achievable with light
  • Fast, due to the lack of motion required for the motors to achieve
  • Low maintenance due to lack of moving parts

Disadvantages

  • The resin is expensive, can cost $100 per litre
  • Machines cost over $2000 for a decent size printer
  • Hard to fix, due to the complexity of SLA printers
